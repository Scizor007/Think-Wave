Understanding Atomic Nuclei

Atoms are the fundamental building blocks of matter, and at their core lies the nucleus, which contains protons and neutrons. The number of protons in the nucleus determines the element, while the number of neutrons influences isotopes of the same element.

In nuclear physics, understanding how protons and neutrons interact helps explain the forces that hold the nucleus together. The strong nuclear force counteracts the repulsion between positively charged protons, ensuring atomic stability.

How the Simulation Works

The "Build a Nucleus" simulation allows you to experiment with creating different atomic nuclei by adding or removing protons and neutrons. Through this interactive tool, you can explore concepts such as nuclear stability, isotopes, and radioactive decay.

By adjusting the number of protons and neutrons, you can observe how atomic nuclei behave under different conditions and learn about fundamental principles of nuclear chemistry and physics.
