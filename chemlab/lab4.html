In 1909, Ernest Rutherford conducted an experiment that led to a groundbreaking discovery about atomic structure. Using alpha particles (helium nuclei) directed at a thin gold foil, he observed how they scattered.

The surprising results showed that:

  • Most alpha particles passed straight through, meaning the atom is mostly empty space.
  • Some particles deflected at angles, suggesting the presence of a dense, positively charged center.
  • A few bounced straight back, proving the existence of a tiny, massive nucleus.
