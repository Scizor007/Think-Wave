Understanding Acids and Bases

Acids and bases are fundamental components of chemistry. Acids, such as vinegar and lemon juice, release hydrogen ions (H⁺) in solution, making them sour and corrosive. Bases, such as baking soda and ammonia, release hydroxide ions (OH⁻), giving them a bitter taste and slippery feel.

The pH scale measures how acidic or basic a solution is, ranging from 0 (strong acid) to 14 (strong base), with 7 being neutral (pure water). This concept is crucial in chemistry, biology, medicine, and industry.
