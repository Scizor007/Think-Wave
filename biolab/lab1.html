Color Vision

Color vision allows organisms to perceive different wavelengths of light as colors. In humans, this process is facilitated by specialized cells in the retina called cones, which detect red, green, and blue light. The brain processes signals from these cones to form a full spectrum of colors.

The perception of color is influenced by factors such as lighting, contrast, and neurological processing. Some animals, like birds and insects, can see a broader spectrum of colors, including ultraviolet light.
