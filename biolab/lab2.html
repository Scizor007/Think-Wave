Neuron

Neurons are the fundamental units of the nervous system, responsible for transmitting information throughout the body. They consist of three main parts: the dendrites (which receive signals), the cell body (which processes information), and the axon (which sends signals to other neurons or muscles).

Neurons communicate via electrical impulses and chemical neurotransmitters at specialized junctions called synapses. This process is essential for brain function, muscle control, and sensory perception.
