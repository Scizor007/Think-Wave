What is a Tesla Coil?

The Tesla Coil is an electrical resonant transformer circuit invented by Nikola Tesla in 1891. It is used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high-frequency alternating current electricity.

Working Principle

The Tesla Coil operates through electromagnetic induction and resonance. It consists of a primary coil, a secondary coil, and a high-voltage transformer. When an alternating current passes through the primary coil, it induces a voltage in the secondary coil, creating high-voltage discharges.

Components of a Tesla Coil

  • Primary Coil: A coil of thick wire that receives the initial charge.
  • Secondary Coil: A longer coil with many turns that steps up the voltage.
  • Capacitor: Stores and releases electrical energy.
  • Discharge Terminal: Releases electrical arcs.

Applications

  • Used in wireless power transmission research.
  • Demonstrations of high-voltage electricity in science exhibits.
  • Predecessor to modern radio transmission technology.
