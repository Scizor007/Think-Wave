What is a Moving Coil Galvanometer?

A moving coil galvanometer is an electromechanical instrument used to detect and measure small electric currents. It operates on the principle of torque acting on a current-carrying coil in a magnetic field.

Working Principle

The device consists of a rectangular coil of wire suspended in a uniform magnetic field. When current flows through the coil, it experiences a torque, causing it to rotate. The deflection is proportional to the current, which can be read using a pointer and scale.

Components of a Moving Coil Galvanometer

  • Coil: A lightweight rectangular coil wound on a non-magnetic frame.
  • Magnetic Field: Provided by a permanent magnet.
  • Suspension: A thin wire or ribbon that provides restoring torque.
  • Pointer & Scale: Displays the deflection of the coil.

Applications

  • Used to measure small electrical currents.
  • Serves as the basis for ammeters and voltmeters.
  • Used in analog measuring instruments.
