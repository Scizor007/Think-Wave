What is an Electromagnetic Wave?

Electromagnetic waves are waves that consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields and propagate through space at the speed of light. These waves do not require a medium to travel and can move through a vacuum.

Characteristics of Electromagnetic Waves

  • They consist of perpendicular electric and magnetic fields.
  • Travel at the speed of light in a vacuum (approximately 3 × 10⁸ m/s).
  • Do not require a medium for propagation.
  • Exhibit wave-particle duality.

Types of Electromagnetic Waves

  • Radio Waves: Used in communication systems.
  • Microwaves: Used in radar and cooking appliances.
  • Infrared Waves: Used in thermal imaging and remote controls.
  • Visible Light: The only part of the spectrum visible to the human eye.
  • Ultraviolet Light: Can cause sunburns and is used in sterilization.
  • X-rays: Used in medical imaging.
  • Gamma Rays: Emitted by radioactive substances and used in cancer treatment.
