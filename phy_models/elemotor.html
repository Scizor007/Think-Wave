What is an Electric Motor?

An electric motor is a device that converts electrical energy into mechanical energy through electromagnetic interactions.

Working Principle

Electric motors operate on the principle of electromagnetism, where a current-carrying conductor placed in a magnetic field experiences a force, causing rotation.

Components of an Electric Motor

  • Stator: The stationary part that generates a magnetic field.
  • Rotor: The moving part that rotates due to electromagnetic forces.
  • Commutator: Helps in switching the direction of current.
  • Brushes: Conduct electricity between the stationary and rotating parts.

Applications

  • Used in household appliances like fans and refrigerators.
  • Essential in electric vehicles for propulsion.
  • Industrial machinery relies on electric motors for automation.
