Plant Cell

Plant Cell Definition

“A plant cell is a eukaryotic cell present in green plants, photosynthetic eukaryotes of the kingdom Plantae. It is distinguished by its rigid cell wall and the presence of chloroplasts.”

Explanation

Plant cells are typically rectangular or square-shaped due to the presence of a rigid cell wall. They play a vital role in photosynthesis and other essential plant functions.

Plant Cell Structure

Plant cells have unique features that differentiate them from animal cells. Some of the key organelles include:

  • Cell Wall: Provides structural support and protection.
  • Cell Membrane: Regulates the entry and exit of substances.
  • Chloroplasts: Contains chlorophyll for photosynthesis.
  • Nucleus: The control center of the cell containing genetic material.
  • Vacuole: Stores nutrients and maintains cell turgor pressure.
  • Mitochondria: Generates energy for the cell.
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Facilitates the transport of materials.
  • Golgi Apparatus: Involved in packaging and transport of proteins.
