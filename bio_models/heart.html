Human Heart Overview

The human heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body, supplying oxygen and nutrients while removing waste products.

Structure and Function

The heart consists of four chambers: two atria and two ventricles. It is divided into left and right sections, each responsible for different circulatory tasks.

Main Components of the Heart

  • Atria: The upper chambers that receive blood.
  • Ventricles: The lower chambers that pump blood out.
  • Valves: Ensure one-way blood flow (tricuspid, pulmonary, mitral, aortic).
  • Arteries and Veins: Transport oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.

Circulatory System

The heart plays a central role in the circulatory system, ensuring blood is continuously supplied to all organs.
