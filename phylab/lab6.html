Projectile Motion

Understanding Projectile Motion

Projectile motion occurs when an object is thrown into the air and moves under the influence of gravity. The motion follows a curved parabolic trajectory, determined by the object's initial speed and launch angle.

Key Equations

  • 1. Time of Flight: T = (2 * v₀ * sinθ) / g
  • 2. Maximum Height: H = (v₀² * sin²θ) / (2g)
  • 3. Range: R = (v₀² * sin(2θ)) / g

    • Where: - v₀ = Initial velocity - θ = Launch angle - g = Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s²)

    Key Concepts

    • Horizontal Motion: Constant velocity, as no horizontal acceleration acts (ignoring air resistance).
    • Vertical Motion: Affected by gravity, causing acceleration downward.
    • Parabolic Path: The combination of horizontal and vertical motions results in a parabola.

    Real-World Applications

    • Sports: Basketball, football, and cricket involve projectile motion.
    • Physics: Motion of cannonballs, rockets, and missiles.
    • Engineering: Designing curved bridges and roller coasters.
