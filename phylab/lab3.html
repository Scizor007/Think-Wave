My Solar System

Exploring Our Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and everything bound to it by gravity—planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The Sun, a massive ball of hot plasma, provides the energy necessary for life on Earth.

Planetary Arrangement

The planets in our Solar System are divided into two groups:

  • Inner Planets (Terrestrial): Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars – Small, rocky, and dense.
  • Outer Planets (Gas Giants): Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune – Large and composed mostly of gas.

Kepler's Laws and Planetary Motion

The movement of planets follows Kepler’s Laws of Motion:

  • Planets orbit the Sun in elliptical paths.
  • They move faster when closer to the Sun and slower when farther away.
  • The farther a planet is, the longer its orbital period.

Fun Facts About Our Solar System

  • Jupiter is the largest planet, while Mercury is the smallest.
  • Venus is the hottest planet, even though Mercury is closest to the Sun.
  • Neptune has the fastest winds, reaching speeds of up to 2,100 km/h.
