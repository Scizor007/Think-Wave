Understanding Faraday’s Law

Faraday’s Law describes how a changing magnetic field can induce an electric current in a conductor. This principle is the foundation of electromagnetic induction, which powers generators, transformers, and electric motors.

Key Concepts

  • Electromagnetic Induction: Moving a magnet through a coil generates electricity.
  • Flux Change: The faster the change in magnetic flux, the stronger the induced current.
  • Direction of Current: Determined by Lenz’s Law, which states that the induced current opposes the change in flux.

Real-World Applications

  • Power generation in hydroelectric dams and wind turbines.
  • Wireless charging of smartphones and electric vehicles.
  • Magnetic levitation (MagLev) trains use this principle for propulsion.
