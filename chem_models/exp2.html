Overview of the Experiment

Millikan's Oil Drop Experiment, conducted by Robert A. Millikan in 1909, was designed to measure the charge of a single electron. This experiment provided strong evidence for the quantization of electric charge.

Setup of the Experiment

  • A fine mist of oil droplets was sprayed into a chamber.
  • The droplets were allowed to fall under gravity between two electrically charged plates.
  • An adjustable electric field was applied to balance the gravitational force on the droplets.
  • By carefully measuring the voltage needed to suspend a droplet, Millikan determined the elementary charge.

Key Observations

  • Droplets moved at different rates when the electric field was adjusted.
  • The charge on each droplet was always a multiple of a fundamental value.
  • The smallest measurable charge was determined to be approximately 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C, the charge of a single electron.

Conclusions and Impact

The experiment confirmed the discrete nature of electric charge and provided a precise measurement of the electron's charge. This was a crucial step in the development of atomic and quantum physics.
